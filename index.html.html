Thesis in a Chart
Conceptual EPS index evolution from domestic infra proxy to diversified global EPC & energy transition platform.

One-line thesis: L&T is migrating from a domestic infra proxy to a diversified global EPC & energy transition platform, with record FY25 order inflows of ₹3.57 tn and a ₹6.67 tn Q2 FY26 order book supporting double-digit growth and structurally higher ROE.

GS Five-Pillar Thesis
  • Structural drivers: multi-year India public capex, international growth, energy transition.
  • Competitive advantage: one of India’s largest order books, multi-discipline EPC, strong subsidiaries.
  • Execution strength: 45% YoY order inflow in Q2 FY26, 10.2% NWC / revenue.
  • Financial outperformance: 16% revenue growth & 11% EBITDA margin in FY25; ROE 16.3%.
  • Valuation support: TP based on blended P/E, EV/EBITDA and DCF; structural premium to peers.

Market Debate vs GS View
Description Block
What the Market Thinks
  • L&T already trades at a premium multiple (≈29x trailing P/E, 5.5x P/B).
  • Infra / EPC is cyclical with execution and commodity risk that may cap upside.
  • International exposure, especially hydrocarbons, could bring geopolitical and margin risk.
GS Variant Perception
  • Current upcycle is more structural, backed by multi-year public capex and energy-transition needs.
  • Order book quality is improving, with higher-value segments, supporting sustainable high-teens ROE.
  • Working capital discipline and asset monetisation should raise FCF and support capital returns.
  • We expect EPS upgrades as Q3–Q4 FY26 confirm order inflow & execution strength.

KPI Glossary
  • Order inflow: new orders booked during the period at contract value.
  • Order book: outstanding unexecuted order value.
  • Book-to-bill: order book divided by annual revenue.
  • EBITDA margin: EBITDA / revenue.
  • ROE: net profit to equity holders / average equity.
  • ROCE: EBIT / capital employed.
  • FCF: operating cash flow minus capex.
  • NWC / revenue: net working capital divided by revenue.
